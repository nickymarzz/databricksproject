OVERVIEW & KEY INFORMATION
================================================================================
Sejong University (SJU; Korean: 세종대학교; Hanja: 世宗大學校) is a private research university located in Seoul, South Korea. It is highly regarded for its standing in hospitality and tourism management, dancing, animation, rhythmic gymnastics, computer science, and artificial intelligence (AI).

Founded as the Kyung Sung Humanities Institute, it was renamed in 1978 in honor of Sejong the Great, the fourth king of the Joseon Dynasty and the overseer of the creation of the Korean alphabet, Hangul. The university features a comprehensive curriculum offering a wide range of undergraduate and graduate programs.

HISTORY
================================================================================
Beginnings (1940–1987):
The history of Sejong University began in May 1940 when the Kyung Sung Humanities Institute was founded by Dr. Youngha Choo and Dr. Okja Choi. In 1947, the institute grew into the Seoul Women's College of Education, with Dr. Choo as its first director. The following year, it was reorganized into a junior college and became the Seoul College of Education for Family and Nursing. 

In 1954, the school became Soodo Women Teachers' College. The college grew into a four-year institution in 1961 and moved to its present campus in Gunja-dong in 1962. In 1966, the Graduate School was established. In 1973, the Soodo Museum (now Sejong University Museum) opened to house the founders' collection of Korean antiquities. In 1979, Soodo Women Teachers' College changed its name to Sejong College and admitted male undergraduates for the first time.

1987–2000:
In October 1987, Sejong College officially transitioned into Sejong University, consisting of five colleges with an enrollment of 6,000 students. In 1996, Dr. Choo Myung-Gun became chairman of the board of trustees. That year saw the establishment of two new graduate schools (Information and Communication, and Education) alongside four new engineering departments (Electronic Engineering, Architectural Engineering, Civil Engineering, and the evening division of Computer Engineering). Ph.D. programs in Hotel Management and Master's programs in Public Administration, Applied Statistics, and Computer Engineering were also introduced. 

By 1997, total enrollment rose to over 7,000, growing by about 1,000 new students each year. In 1998, the College of Tourism and the Department of Biological Engineering were founded. Construction began on five new buildings, including the library, the chapel, and engineering laboratories. 

In 1999, three new graduate schools were added: Software Engineering, Science and Technology, and Performance Art Administration, while the Materials Engineering department was established. A cooperative agreement with Lockheed Martin Aerospace led to the foundation of the Sejong-Lockheed Martin Aerospace Research Center. Similar tech-development partnerships were formed with Hanaro Communication, Hansol PCS, Onse Communication, and Prime Company.

In 2000, the Graduate Schools of Film Art and Techno Design were established. The Graduate School of Business Administration was renamed the Graduate School of Global Business Administration, and the College of Tourism became the College of Hotels and Tourism. The new 12-story Library opened, housing over 400,000 volumes, making it the first WTO repository library in Korea.

2001–Present:
In 2001, a collaborative relationship was established with the Korea Science and Culture Foundation. Two new English-taught programs were opened: an M.A. in Asian Studies and a Global M.B.A. operated jointly with Syracuse University in the United States. In January 2002, a University Development Advisory Board composed of CEOs from 30 major Korean firms was established to strengthen industry ties. 

The Education Reform Deliberation Commission awarded Sejong University for successful restructuring and renovation in 2002. In 2004, the Ministry of Education & Human Resources Development recognized the university for excellence in originality, innovation, and academic achievement. In 2007, the Graduate School of Business received formal certification from the Association to Advance Collegiate Schools of Business (AACSB).

AFFILIATED INSTITUTIONS
================================================================================
Sejong University Library:
Established in 1947 and renovated into a 12-story building in 2000, the library houses 840,000 books, 1,500 journal subscriptions, 20,000 e-journals, and 23,000 e-books. It contains reading rooms with 3,200 seats, group study areas, and operates as the first WTO repository library in South Korea.

Continuing Education Center:
Provides open and lifelong learning opportunities, complementing the regular university curricula. It runs programs such as the Sejong Global Program for Studying Abroad, a Specialized Casino Dealer Course, Digital Contents, Culinary and Food Service Business, Hotel Business, Physical Education, and Music (Piano). It also offers a bachelor's degree program based on South Korea's Academic Credit Bank System.

Information Services & Technology Center (ISTC):
Provides information technology support and services for students, faculty, and staff. This includes the maintenance of wired and wireless network infrastructures, data protection for academic systems, and the administration of the unified university information system.

The Sejong International Language Institute:
Offers language programs across three core languages:
* Korean: Six proficiency levels ranging from beginner to advanced.
* English: English for Specific Purposes, English for Global Communication Skills in Business, and English for Global Leaders.
* Chinese: Chinese Character classes and Chinese for Business Communication.

* College of Natural Sciences: Mathematics and Statistics, Physics and Astronomy, Chemistry.
* College of Life Sciences: Food Science and Biotechnology, Integrative Bioscience and Biotechnology, Bioresources Engineering.
* College of AI Convergence & Engineering / Arts and Physical Education: (Programs extending into specialized concentrations like Computer Science, Mechanical Engineering, Animation, Dance, and Rhythmic Gymnastics).
